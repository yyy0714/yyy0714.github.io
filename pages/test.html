Threshold Voltage $V_{\rm t}$

Threshold voltage is the value of gate-source voltage ($v_{\rm GS}$) at which a sufficient number of mobile electrons accumulate in the channel region to form a conducting channel.

Overdrive Voltage $v_{\rm ov}$

\begin{equation*} v_{\rm ov} \equiv v_{\rm GS} - V_{\rm t} \end{equation*}
$ V_{\rm t} $
Threshold voltage
$ \rm V $

Oxide Capacitance $C_{\rm ox}$

Oxide capacitance is the capacitance of the parallel-plate capacitor per unit area.

\begin{equation*} C_{\rm ox} = \frac{\epsilon_{\rm ox}}{t_{\rm ox}} \end{equation*}
$\epsilon_{\rm ox}$
Oxide permittivity
$\rm F/m$
$t_{\rm ox}$
Oxide thickness
$\rm m$

Channel charge $Q$

\begin{equation} Q = C_{\rm ox} \cdot W \cdot L \cdot v_{\rm ov} \label{eq:channel-charge} \end{equation}
$ W $
Channel width
$ \rm m $
$ L $
Channel length
$ \rm m $

Assumptions

For the following sections, a few assumptions are made:
  • Enhancement-type n-channel MOSFET is used
  • Source and base are connected to the gound ($v_{\rm S} = v_{\rm B} = 0$)
  • $v_{\rm GS}$ is held constant and $v_{\rm GS} > V_t$
  • $v_{\rm DS}$ is positive, so electrons flow from source to drain

Triode Region: Small $v_{\rm DS}$

Since $v_{\rm DS}$ is small, we can assume that the voltage between the gate and various points along the channel is uniform and equal to $v_{\rm GS}$.

According to Coloumb's law, the electric field $\mathbf{E}$ across the channel is \begin{equation*} |\mathbf{E}| = \frac{v_{\rm DS}}{L} \end{equation*}

According to Ohm's law, the drift velocity $\mathbf{u}$ of the channel electrons towards the drain is \begin{equation} |\mathbf{u}| = \mu_{\rm n} |\mathbf{E}| \label{eq:channel-electron-velocity} \end{equation}

$\mu_{\rm n}$
Electron mobility
$\rm m^2/(V \cdot s)$

According to equation \eqref{eq:channel-charge} and equation \eqref{eq:channel-electron-velocity}, the channel current $i_{\rm D}$ is simply \begin{aligned} i_{\rm D} &= |\mathbf{u}| \cdot \frac{Q}{L} \\ &= \mu_{\rm n} \cdot C_{\rm ox} \cdot \frac{W}{L} \cdot v_{\rm ov} \cdot v_{\rm DS} \end{aligned}

Observe that $i_{\rm D}$ is linearly dependent on $v_{\rm DS}$, thus the MOSFET is operating like a resistor with resistance \begin{equation*} r_{\rm DS} = \frac{1}{\mu_{\rm n} \cdot C_{\rm ox} \cdot \frac{W}{L} \cdot v_{\rm ov}} \end{equation*}

Saturation Region: Large $v_{\rm DS}$

As $v_{\rm DS}$ increases, we can no longer assume that the voltage between the gate and various points along the channel is uniform and equal to $v_{\rm GS}$. Instead, the voltage of interest near the source now is now $v_{\rm GS} - v_{\rm DS}$.
